Pitta granatina, the garnet pitta, is a bird in the same genus as the blue headed pitta, and with the information available, their niches are almost identical. Its diet consists of small insects, grubs, worms, and sometimes seeds and snails, and is most sexually active during the middle of the year. Like the Blue headed pitta, it inhabits the lowland rainforests of borneo, where it is most common, sumatra, and the mainland portion of malaysia. Likewise, due to high deforestation rates, it is also endangered and losing habitat.
