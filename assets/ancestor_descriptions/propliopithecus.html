Propliopithecus is an extinct genus of ape. The 40 cm (1 ft 4 in) long creature resembled today's gibbons. Its eyes faced forwards, giving it stereoscopical vision. Propliopithecus was most likely an omnivore. The fossil record of higher primates in the Old World begins in the Oligocene of Egypt and extend through the Miocene, Pliocene, and Pleistocene of East Africa, Europe, and Asia.
